My Journey as a Senior QA Analyst

Embarking on a career in Quality Assurance has been a rewarding journey of growth, learning, and discovery. Over the years, I’ve honed my skills in ensuring software excellence, collaborating with diverse teams, and adapting to ever-evolving technologies. From mastering manual testing techniques to leading automation initiatives with tools like Playwright and Postman, my path has been driven by a passion for delivering seamless user experiences. Each project has not only strengthened my technical expertise but also deepened my understanding of the critical role QA plays in the software development lifecycle. This is a reflection of the challenges, milestones, and lessons that have shaped me into the Senior QA Analyst I am today.

QA Strategy Principles

The best QA strategy depends on the context of the project, but an effective strategy typically involves a mix of planning, execution, communication, and continuous improvement.


  • Shift Left: Start testing early in the development cycle to catch defects sooner.
  • User-Centric Approach: Test from the perspective of the end-user to ensure usability and satisfaction.
  • Test Early, Test Often: Adopt Agile and DevOps principles to ensure continuous testing throughout the lifecycle.

By following these steps and customizing them to your project’s needs, you can build a QA strategy that ensures high-quality deliverables and aligns with business objectives.

Importance of SDLC

The Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) is crucial for ensuring the systematic and efficient development of high-quality software.


  • SDLC breaks down the software development process into manageable phases, ensuring a clear roadmap from initiation to deployment. It identifies and eliminates defects, leading to a stable, reliable, and high-performing product.
  • SDLC is the backbone of effective software development. It provides a structured, repeatable, and scalable process that ensures quality, reduces risks, and aligns software solutions with business objectives. By following the SDLC, teams can deliver successful projects that meet user expectations and business goals.
